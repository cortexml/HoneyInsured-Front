Know Your Choices: 4 Options for 2016 Health Insurance

October 28, 2015 | Grace Gee

What are the differences between on exchange (Obamacare) plans, off exchange plans, short term plans and going uninsured? Get a qualified health plans to avoid federal tax penalties. If you are not covered by your job, these are your 4 options:

  1. On-Exchange Plan
    • You can find On-Exchange Plans at https://honeyinsured.com or Healthcare.gov.
    • These plans are subject to strict standards and must cover preventative care, pre-existing conditions and essential health benefits (including pregnancy, prescription drug, birth control, and emergency)**.
    • You must enroll in On-Exchange Plan between November 1, 2015 and January 31, 2016. On other dates, you can enroll if you have a qualifying event*.
    • On exchange plans are eligible for government help in tax subsidies and cost-sharing.
  2. Off-Exchange Plan
    • Off-Exchange Plans, like On-Exchange Plans, have the same enrollment deadlines and strict coverage standards.
    • You cannot enroll in Off-Exchange Plans on Healthcare.gov. However, we show and can enroll you in select Off-Exchange Plans.
    • Unlike On-Exchange plans, you cannot get tax subsidies and cost-sharing help.
  3. Short-Term Plan
    • Short-term plans are not subject to the strict standards as Off-Exchange and On-Exchange plans. That means that the insurance company can choose to not cover your pre-existing conditions. You can also be denied coverage for non-health reasons.
    • You can enroll in a short-term plan any time during the year.
    • Short-term plans also do not exempt you from the tax penalty.
  4. Uninsured
    • You can always choose to go uninsured or without coverage. Going uninsured means that you’ll pay a tax penalty and for all your medical expenses.
    • The tax penalty for 2016 is the greater of 1) $695/adult, $347.50/child (family max $2,085) or 2) 2.5% of your household income that is above the tax return filing threshold for your filing status.
